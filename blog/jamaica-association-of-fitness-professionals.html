Building a Stronger, Safer Fitness Industry: The Jamaica Association of Fitness Professionals (JAFP)

By Master Bryan Kukibo | April 18, 2026

When we talk about health and wellness in Jamaica, we often focus on the individual — the person lacing up their sneakers, hitting the gym, or hiring a personal trainer to help them reach their goals. But behind every great fitness journey is a professional guiding the way. And that professional needs to be qualified, knowledgeable, and accountable.

That is exactly the vision that gave birth to the Jamaica Association of Fitness Professionals (JAFP) — an organization founded by fitness pioneer Bryan Campbell, widely known as Master Bryan — and one that has been quietly but powerfully shaping the landscape of fitness in Jamaica since 2006.

The Man Behind the Mission

Master Bryan Campbell is not just a fitness professional — he is a visionary. With a deep passion for the health and wellness of the Jamaican people, and a clear understanding of the gaps within the local fitness industry, Master Bryan took action. In 2006, he founded the Jamaica Association of Fitness Professionals with a bold and necessary goal: to bring structure, accountability, and excellence to an industry that desperately needed it.

His work with the JAFP is a direct reflection of his lifelong commitment to not only transforming individual bodies, but transforming an entire industry — and by extension, the health of a nation.

What Is the JAFP?

The Jamaica Association of Fitness Professionals is a body dedicated to uniting fitness professionals across Jamaica under one common standard — excellence. Founded on the belief that the fitness industry, like medicine, law, or education, must be governed by rules and regulations that protect the public and elevate the profession.

At its core, the JAFP advocates for a simple but critical principle: anyone who trains another person should be certified to do so. No exceptions.

Why Regulation Matters in Fitness

Jamaica's fitness industry has grown enormously over the years. Gyms, bootcamps, online coaches, and personal trainers are everywhere — and that growth is exciting. But with growth comes risk. Not every person calling themselves a fitness trainer has the education or qualifications to safely guide someone through a workout program, especially clients with health conditions, injuries, or special needs.

An uncertified trainer can cause serious harm — from poor programming that leads to injury, to dangerous advice around nutrition and supplementation. The JAFP exists to close that gap.

By pushing for mandatory certification standards, the JAFP is protecting everyday Jamaicans who place their trust — and their bodies — in the hands of fitness professionals. This is not about gatekeeping. This is about accountability, safety, and respect for the people we serve.

The Positive Impact of the JAFP

Here are some of the key ways the JAFP is making a difference in Jamaica's fitness and wellness landscape:

1. Raising the Standard of Professionalism

The JAFP sets a benchmark for what it means to be a fitness professional in Jamaica. By encouraging and advocating for certification, the association ensures that trainers have a solid foundation in anatomy, exercise science, program design, and client safety. This raises the overall quality of fitness services across the island.

2. Protecting the Public

When a client walks into a gym or hires a personal trainer, they deserve to know that the person they are working with is qualified. The JAFP's push for mandatory certification means that Jamaicans can have greater confidence in the professionals they choose to trust with their health.

3. Creating a Community of Fitness Professionals

One of the greatest strengths of the JAFP is the community it builds. Fitness professionals across Jamaica — trainers, instructors, coaches, and wellness experts — come together under one umbrella, sharing knowledge, supporting one another, and growing the industry collectively. There is immense power in unity.

4. Educating Through Seminars and Workshops

The JAFP does not just set standards — it actively helps people meet them. Through regular seminars and workshops, the association provides aspiring and existing fitness professionals with access to quality education and training. These events cover topics ranging from how to become a certified fitness instructor, to the latest trends in health and wellness, to best practices in client management and safety.

These workshops are particularly valuable for young Jamaicans who are passionate about fitness and want to turn that passion into a legitimate career. The JAFP provides a pathway.

5. Growing the Fitness Industry as a Whole

A stronger, more regulated fitness industry is good for everyone. When professionals are certified and held to high standards, client results improve, trust increases, and more people feel confident investing in their health and wellness. The JAFP's work helps grow the entire ecosystem — creating more jobs, more opportunities, and more healthy Jamaicans.

6. Contributing to the Health and Wellness of the Nation

Jamaica faces significant public health challenges, including rising rates of non-communicable diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, and obesity. A well-trained, professional fitness industry is one of the most powerful tools we have in fighting these conditions. The JAFP understands this, and its mission is deeply tied to the health and wellness of the Jamaican nation as a whole.

A Vision Worth Supporting

Since 2006, Master Bryan Campbell and the JAFP have been doing the quiet, necessary work of building a fitness industry that Jamaica can be proud of. It is an organization driven not by profit, but by purpose — the belief that fitness professionals have a responsibility to the people they serve and to the nation they call home.

I encourage every fitness professional in Jamaica — whether you are just starting out or have years of experience — to align yourself with this association. Get certified. Attend the workshops. Join the community. Be part of the movement that Master Bryan started.

And to the public: when you are choosing a personal trainer or fitness instructor, ask about their qualifications. You deserve the best. The JAFP is working to make sure you get it.
